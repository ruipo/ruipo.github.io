Precipice - Acadia
  • Back to Hiking Summary

November 11, 2023

The Precipice Trail gave us a thrill! At only 3.2 miles roundtrip but with over 1000 feet of elevation gain, the hike features many sections of switchbacks, exposed rocky ledges, and iron ladders up the cliff faces. We had heard a lot about this hike before and arrived early in the day expecting to beat the crowds. It turns out however, that Acadia is not that popular in mid-November (pro-tip, a great time to visit, as long as you’re fine with chilly temps!) and we pretty much had the trail to ourselves.

Fortunately, there were still remnants of leaves and fall colors around, without any snowfall yet. Our gloves came in handy for this hike as we ascended iron rungs and ladders and crossed small bridges clinging to the cliff edges. The views of the Atlantic Ocean were gorgeous against the late fall colors surrounding us. This hike was the perfect level of adventure for us!

Scenes from the hike: Christine climbing the iron rungs; Rui on another similar trail - the Beehive - overlooking Sand Beach; ocean views beyond; trail signage; Rui amongst the fall colors; views of Acadia’s landscape and islands beyond.

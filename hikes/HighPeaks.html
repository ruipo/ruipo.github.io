High Peaks - Pinnacles
  • Back to Hiking Summary

September 10, 2023

This hike had a bit of everything and surprised us with its beauty. The total hiking distance was about 6 miles, with 1300 feet of elevation gain. We began the hike through the Bear Gulch Cave Trail, including weaving our way through huge talus rock formations (caves, essentially) - our flashlights definitely came in handy for this.

We were also treated to a beautiful sunrise and had much of the trail to ourselves. Fortunately, we completed the ascent before the day got too hot. Although we didn’t spot any California condors, we did see a number of turkey vultures circling near the top of the trail, which were an impressive sight.

The best feature of this hike was the steep sections built directly into the rock overlooking the canyons. Notable critters spotted in Pinnacles included two wild tarantulas!

Scenes from the hike: some of the namesake volcanic pinnacles formations; several pictures of the grand view from the top; the Bear Gulch Reservoir.

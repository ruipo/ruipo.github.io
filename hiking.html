It started with a trip to the American southwest in 2021. The grand landscapes of Zion and Yosemite sparked our passion for hiking. Since then, we have been deliberate in spending more time together exploring the outdoors and reveling in the simplicity of one foot in front of the other. 2023 was no exception - from rock scrambling in Shenandoah to cave touring in Kentucky, from exploring trails near our home, to climbing the strenuous Half Dome in Yosemite, we spent a total of 131 hours on the trails this past year - that's about 5 and 1/2 days of hiking! Time well spent.

Thanks to a nifty GoPro (thanks Uncle Bob and Aunt Karen!) and a smart-watch, we were able to document our hikes and create a repository of the highlights to share with family and friends, as well as with our future selves. We invite you to check out some of our favorites below!

In total, we hiked more than 426 miles in 2023. In January, we took an impromptu trip to Shenandoah to hike Old Rag. This 9-mile hike accounted for nearly all of our hiking that month and was a blast to do. We stayed inside for most of the winter and emerged again in the spring. The rest of the year primarily revolved around preparing ourselves to hike Half Dome in September. To train, we went on several long (15+ miles) hikes around our home during the months of July and August. These greatly boosted our mileage, and we explored many paths and neighborhoods around us that we would not have otherwise. Then, in September came Half Dome, which exceeded all of our expectations and added about 5000 ft of elevation gain to our tally! In the same trip, we also hiked a great deal in Pinnacles, Sequoia, and Kings Canyon National Parks. We finished the year with a visit to Acadia in November and completed one of the more adventurous hikes we've done with the Precipice Trail.

We're thankful for the time we were able to spend together on the trails, and for friends and family who hosted us during the year. We look forward to more memories in 2024!
